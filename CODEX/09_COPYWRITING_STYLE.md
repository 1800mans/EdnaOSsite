# EDNA OS Website
# Copywriting Style
## Version 1.0

---

# Purpose

This document defines the editorial voice and writing style for the EDNA OS website.

Its purpose is to ensure that every page, article and engineering document sounds as if it were written by the same organization.

The writing should communicate engineering credibility, operational experience and long-term thinking.

It should never resemble startup marketing.

---

# Writing Philosophy

The website documents an engineering project.

It does not advertise software.

The objective is to educate, explain and document.

Readers should finish each page believing they have learned something rather than having been sold something.

---

# Brand Voice

The EDNA OS voice is:

- Engineering
- Honest
- Precise
- Calm
- Practical
- Confident
- Evidence-driven
- Long-term
- Transparent

Never:

- Excited
- Aggressive
- Promotional
- Sales-oriented
- Overconfident
- Sensational

---

# Tone

The preferred tone resembles:

Engineering documentation

Technical field reports

Research publications

Industrial manuals

Operational journals

Commercial agriculture

The tone should never resemble:

Startup websites

Marketing brochures

Investor presentations

Product advertisements

Technology blogs

---

# Writing Principles

Every paragraph should satisfy at least one of the following:

Explain

Teach

Document

Measure

Clarify

Provide context

Support with evidence

If a paragraph does none of these, remove it.

# Avoid Absolute Claims

Do not write

"This is the best..."

"This guarantees..."

"This solves..."

"This eliminates..."

Instead write

"This is designed to..."

"This aims to..."

"This is intended to..."

"This allows..."

"This makes it possible to..."

"This is currently being evaluated..."

---

# Evidence First

Support conclusions with engineering reasoning.

Instead of

"Our irrigation system is better."

Write

"Operational evidence collected from lysimeters is intended to improve irrigation decisions over time."

---

# Confidence Without Hype

Good

"The system is being validated under commercial orchard conditions."

Bad

"The system is transforming agriculture."

# Human Language

Write naturally.

Avoid unnecessary complexity.

Prefer

"The PLC controls irrigation sequencing."

Instead of

"The programmable logic controller facilitates deterministic irrigation sequencing functionality."

Simple engineering language is stronger than academic language.

---

# Sentence Length

Prefer short and medium sentences.

Mix sentence lengths naturally.

Avoid long paragraphs.

Most paragraphs should contain

2–5 sentences.

---

# Headings

---

# First Person

Use first person sparingly.

Preferred

"The project is being developed..."

Occasionally acceptable

"We are evaluating..."

Avoid

"We are excited..."

"We proudly present..."

"We believe..."

---

# Second Person

Use "you" only when appropriate.

Avoid making the website conversational.

Instead of

"You can revolutionize your orchard."

Write

"Commercial orchards require coordinated operational control."

---

# Technical Accuracy

Do not simplify engineering concepts to the point of becoming inaccurate.

If a concept is complex:

Introduce it.

Explain it.

Link to a dedicated page.

---

# Explain Before Naming

Introduce the problem first.

Then introduce terminology.

Example

Most irrigation systems measure how much water is applied.

EDNA is designed to measure what reaches the root zone.

This approach is referred to as **Operational Evidence**.

---

# Repetition

Strategic repetition is encouraged.

Certain ideas should appear throughout the website.

Examples

Engineering before software.

Technology serves the orchard.

Operational evidence.

Deterministic control.

Commercial orchard.

Continuous validation.

These phrases reinforce the project's identity.

---

# Numbers

Use real measurements whenever possible.

Examples

10 irrigation zones

1.5 kW VFD

24 V DC valves

1 m² lysimeter

140 m irrigation run

Concrete numbers build credibility.

---

# Photography References

Whenever referring to an image,

explain its engineering significance.

Not

"Pump station"

Instead

"The irrigation pump station serves as the primary hydraulic control point for the orchard."

---

# Founder Story

Avoid heroic narratives.

The story should emphasize necessity.

Example

"EDNA OS began as a practical engineering response to the operational challenges of managing a commercial orchard."

Not

"Our founder had a bold vision to transform agriculture."

---

# Research Mindset

The website should communicate that engineering is ongoing.

Use phrases such as

Current validation

Field observations

Initial findings

Ongoing evaluation

Future investigation

Working hypothesis

Engineering revision

Continuous improvement

---

# Failure

Do not hide engineering failures.

If appropriate, describe

Problem

Investigation

Root Cause

Correction

Lessons Learned

Transparency builds credibility.

---

# Consistency

Use the same terminology throughout the website.

Always use

Orchard Operating System

Never alternate with

Orchard Platform

Agriculture OS

Farm OS

Tree Operating System

Choose one term and remain consistent.

---

# Abbreviations

Spell out terms the first time they appear.

Example

Programmable Logic Controller (PLC)

Variable Frequency Drive (VFD)

Electrical Conductivity (EC)

Afterward,

use the abbreviation consistently.

---

# Lists

Use bullet lists for

Objectives

Principles

Specifications

Benefits

Requirements

Avoid long paragraphs containing multiple ideas.

---

# Quotes

Avoid inspirational quotes.

The only acceptable recurring statements are engineering principles.

Examples

Engineering precedes software.

Measurement precedes optimization.

Every irrigation cycle is a controlled experiment.

Operational evidence improves future decision logic.

---

# Final Paragraphs

Most pages should conclude by naturally leading to the next engineering concept.

Example

Understanding irrigation control is only part of the system.

The next section explains how EDNA measures the outcomes of every irrigation cycle through operational evidence.

---

# Final Statement

The EDNA OS website should read like the public engineering notebook of a long-term research project.

Every sentence should increase technical credibility.

Every page should teach.

Every paragraph should earn the reader's trust.

The objective is not persuasion.

The objective is understanding.

Engineering speaks for itself.
