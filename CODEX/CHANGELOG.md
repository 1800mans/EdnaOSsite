This document records major architectural, documentation and design decisions for the EDNA OS website.

The changelog tracks the evolution of the engineering specification rather than individual source code commits.

# Versioning Policy

Documentation follows semantic versioning.

```
Major.Minor
```

Examples

```
1.0

1.1

1.2

2.0
```

Major versions represent significant architectural changes.

Minor versions represent additions or refinements that do not alter the overall project direction.

## July 2026

Status

Approved

## Milestone

Initial Engineering Specification Completed

---

## Major Decisions

### Website Positioning

Approved

The website represents an engineering project.

It is intentionally **not** presented as:

- SaaS
- Startup
- Product landing page
- Marketing website

---

### Engineering Category

Approved

The website is dedicated to defining the engineering discipline:

**Orchard Operating System**

This concept becomes the foundation of the entire project.

---

### Engineering Philosophy

Approved

The website is built around the principles:

- Engineering before software
- Architecture before implementation
- Measurement before optimization
- Operational evidence before conclusions
- Technology serves orchard performance

---

### Visual Direction

Approved

Design language

Industrial Agriculture

combined with

Automation

and

Built-in Research

Rejected directions

- AgTech marketing
- SaaS aesthetics
- Futuristic AI interfaces
- Neon themes
- Generic dashboards

---

### Photography Strategy

Approved

Only real engineering photography should be used.

Primary subjects include:

- Commercial orchards
- Pump stations
- PLC cabinets
- Irrigation infrastructure
- Electrical systems
- Sensors
- Lysimeters
- Root systems
- Field engineering

AI-generated imagery is prohibited for production.

### Core Engineering Message

Approved

The recurring engineering statement is

> Every irrigation cycle is a controlled experiment.

> Every experiment produces operational evidence.

> Operational evidence improves future decision logic.

This concept should appear consistently throughout the website.

---

### Second Core Innovation

Approved

The website distinguishes EDNA from conventional irrigation systems through the principle:

Most systems measure inputs.

EDNA measures outcomes.

This concept becomes the foundation of the Measuring Outcomes section.

---

### Lysimeter System

Approved

Lysimeters are identified as one of the strongest engineering differentiators.

Dedicated documentation and future expansion have been planned.

---

### Deployment Strategy

Approved

Deployment refers to engineering validation on a commercial orchard.

It does not refer to customer installations.

---

# Project Vision

EDNA OS is intended to become the authoritative engineering reference for the Orchard Operating System.

The website will evolve alongside the engineering project, documenting not only the final system, but also the architecture, field validation, operational evidence and engineering decisions that shaped its development.

This changelog records that evolution.
